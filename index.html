What is Trailhead?

Salesforce Trailhead is a free learning platform used by millions of professionals to get certified and build careers in enterprise software. Through guided modules, hands-on challenges, and community forums, it takes beginners to certified experts.

Project Description

Over 10 months, our team partnered with the Salesforce Trailhead product team to research, design, and prototype new features for the platform's most experienced users — the power users who built their careers on Trailhead and then quietly stopped coming back.

Trailhead was built for the climb, and not for what comes after.

As users become experts, Trailhead use shifts from continuous learning to occasional, task-driven engagement — until it stops entirely.

"How might we drive advanced users to keep returning to Trailhead with deeper, relevant, and career-aligned experiences?"

🏆

Certifications are the only universal motivator

"Badges and the Ranger tag — not that much. Certifications do help."

📉

Content gets shallow too fast

"You learn in auto-roto mode, your brain does not work. Things are cookie cutter and too basic."

🔀

The community has migrated away

"On Trailhead you get a response in 48-72 hrs. On Slack you get it by afternoon."

🔔

Usage is reactive, not habitual

Users return only when triggered by a project or deadline — not from any pull the platform creates.

🤖

Users supplement Trailhead without guilt

Going to ChatGPT first and following Trailhead links it surfaces. Nobody apologised.

📈

Contribution lifecycle the platform ignores

Users move from consuming → answering → speaking at conferences. Trailhead only supports the first two.

Ideation

From constraints to concepts

Ideation began with five engagement requirement areas: post-milestone engagement, career visibility, habit-forming systems, community integration, and real-world problem solving. We used these as constraints — not prompts — to find the smallest intervention that would create a genuine reason to return.

Team ideation sessions — whiteboard synthesis, affinity mapping, concept development

Solution

Reimagined
Trailhead.

Reimagined as an experience that builds real-world readiness, keeping expert users engaged beyond milestones.

Feature One

Trailhead Solve

An AI chatbot that answers real work questions and nudges users to trails.

Describe a real Salesforce problem in plain language. Get a structured, expert answer calibrated to your certification level and Salesforce release — not a list of trails to complete. Includes expertise toggle, version-specific flagging, and a community escalation path.

Feature Two

Adaptive Dashboard

A homepage that learns from you and tells you what to do next.

An adaptive career layer that tells you what changed, where you stand, and what to do next. One personalised action — never a list — tied to certifications, recent Solve sessions, and release changes. Each visit becomes more valuable than the last.

📸 assets/screenshots/adaptive-dashboard.png

How does it work?

1

Type your problem in plain language. Get a specific, actionable answer from Salesforce's full knowledge base — calibrated to your expertise level.

2

Adjust the answer depth to Novice, Intermediate, or Professional — so you're never over-explained or underserved.

3

Still stuck? Solve drafts a Trailblazer Community post for you, pre-filled and ready to publish — so you get an expert answer either way.

Testing & Impact

Did it work?

We were not measuring task completion speed — we were measuring whether the product creates a new motivation to learn.

Internal

AI-Simulated Stress Testing

We built 12 AI agents from our research data to simulate power user behavior and stress-test prototype flows before any real participant encountered them.

External

Moderated Think-Aloud Sessions

8 Salesforce power users, ~30 minutes each. Where participants would normally use ChatGPT, we asked them to try Solve first — giving us a live substitution signal.

6/8

Would use Trailhead Solve over ChatGPT for Salesforce-specific questions

4.5/5

Average confidence rating in Solve's answers

5/8

Said the Dashboard surfaced insights they wouldn't have explored on their own

6/8

Expressed unprompted return intent after the session

Reflection

What we learned building this

What worked

Focusing on power users who had already arrived gave every design decision a clear test. If a feature could apply to a beginner, it was wrong. That constraint produced sharper work than any open-ended prompt could have.

What was hard

Recruiting authentic power users within a semester timeline is genuinely difficult. These are full-time professionals across five time zones who have no particular reason to give a student team 30 minutes.

What we'd do next

Scale testing globally with certified professionals. Refine Solve answer quality using structured feedback loops. Pitch an integration roadmap to Salesforce Trailhead's product team.
